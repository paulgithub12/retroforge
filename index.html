About This Website

This website explains the key Elements of Design used in visual communication and digital design. Each section explains an element, shows an example image, and includes an avatar video describing the concept.

Line

Lines guide the viewer's eye and create structure in designs. They can be straight, curved, thick, thin, or dashed. Lines are used to separate content and create shapes.

Shape

Shapes are formed when lines connect together. Common shapes include circles, squares, and triangles. Shapes help organize design layouts and create visual interest.

Colour

Colour communicates emotion and meaning. Designers use colour to attract attention, create contrast, and establish mood within a design.

Texture

Texture refers to the surface quality of an object. In digital design it gives the appearance of depth and realism, making elements look smooth, rough, or patterned.
